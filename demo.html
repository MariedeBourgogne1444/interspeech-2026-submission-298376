DiaMoE-TTS: A Unified IPA-based Framework
for Low-Resource Dialect Text-to-Speech

Anonymous Authors
Submitted to Interspeech 2026

Abstract

Dialect speech embodies rich cultural and linguistic diversity, yet building a unified text-to-speech (TTS) system for dialects remains challenging due to scarce data, inconsistent phonetic representations, and dialect interference in joint modeling. We explore a unified dialect TTS pipeline based on a Diffusion Transformer (DiT) architecture. A unified International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA) front-end is adopted to reduce pronunciation ambiguity across dialects. Built upon the F5-TTS framework, we introduce a dialect-aware Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) text encoder to mitigate style averaging in multidialect training. For low-resource dialect expansion, we employ parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) to enable adaptation with only a few hours of speech data. We further investigate incorporating high-quality dialect data into the unified model. Experiments show that GRPO-based reinforcement learning reduces the word error rate (WER) for both the target dialect and several related dialects, outperforming simple retraining with the same data. Our work provides a reproducible baseline for scalable multidialect TTS under open-data conditions.
